The \(P\)-value, 0.0127, tells us it is "unlikely" that we would observe such an extreme test statistic \(t^*\) in the direction of \(H_A\) if the null hypothesis were true. Therefore, our initial assumption that the null hypothesis is true must be incorrect. That is, since the \(P\)-value, 0.0127, is less than \(\alpha = 0.05\text{,}\) we reject the null hypothesis \(H_0 : \mu = 3\) in favor of the alternative hypothesis \(H_A : \mu > 3\text{.}\)
