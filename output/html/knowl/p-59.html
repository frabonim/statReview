In the next section, we review the p-value procedure for determining if a result is unlikely. To make our review concrete, let's imagine that \(\mu\) is the average grade point average of all American students who major in mathematics. We first review the critical value approach for conducting each of the following three hypothesis tests about the population mean \(\mu\text{:}\)

Type Null Alternative
Right-tailed \(H_0: \mu = 3\) \(H_A: \mu > 3\)
Left-tailed \(H_0: \mu = 3\) \(H_A: \mu < 3\)
Two-tailed \(H_0: \mu = 3\) \(H_A: \mu \neq 3\)
